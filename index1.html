The Milky Way

  1. Its Structure

    • Disk: The Milky Way has a flattened, rotating disk with a central bar. The Sun is located in one of the spiral arms, about 27,000 light-years from the galactic center.
    • Bulge: At the center of the galaxy, there's a dense, spherical region known as the bulge, which contains older stars.
    • Halo: Surrounding the disk is a halo, which consists of older stars and globular clusters. The halo also likely contains a significant amount of dark matter.
    • Galactic Center:At the heart of the Milky Way lies a supermassive black hole called Sagittarius A*, with a mass of around 4 million times that of the Sun.

  2. Size and scale:

    • The Milky Way is vast, with a diameter of about 100,000 light-years and containing billions of stars.
    • Its mass is estimated to be around 100 billion times that of the Sun.

  3. Components:

    • Stars: The Milky Way is home to a diverse population of stars, ranging from massive, hot blue stars to smaller, cooler red stars, and most importantly,our solar system
    • Gas and Dust: Interstellar medium, composed of gas and dust, fills the regions between stars. This material is the birthplace of new stars.
    • Dark Matter: Although invisible, dark matter is believed to make up a significant portion of the Milky Way's mass. Its gravitational influence helps hold the galaxy together.

The Solar System

The center of the Solar System is the Sun. The Solar System is made up of the Sun and all the planets, asteroids, and other objects that orbit the Sun.

The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star, commonly referred to as a yellow dwarf.

  1. Composition: The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74% by mass) and helium (about 24%). It also contains trace amounts of heavier elements.
  2. Energy Source: The Sun generates energy through nuclear fusion in its core, where hydrogen nuclei combine to form helium, releasing vast amounts of energy in the process.
  3. Size and Mass: The Sun has a diameter of about 1.4 million kilometers (870,000 miles), making it about 109 times the diameter of Earth. Its mass is approximately 333,000 times that of Earth.
  4. Temperature: The core temperature is about 15 million degrees Celsius (27 million degrees Fahrenheit), while the surface temperature (photosphere) is around 5,500 degrees Celsius (9,932 degrees Fahrenheit).
  5. Lifespan: The Sun is approximately 4.6 billion years old and is middle-aged. It is expected to continue its current phase of hydrogen fusion for another 5 billion years.
